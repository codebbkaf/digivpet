## Codebase Patterns
- SIMULATOR SCREENSHOTS: `export DEVELOPER_DIR=/Applications/Xcode_26_4_1.app/Contents/Developer`,
  build once against Series 11 (46mm, watchOS 26.4), then `simctl install` the SAME .app onto the
  41mm Series 9 (watchOS 10.4) — deployment target is 10.0 and Series 9 is not a usable
  `xcodebuild -destination`. Sim UDIDs: 46mm BA425DD4-8856-4933-8198-0E4B89960E48,
  41mm FB045EDB-DEB9-40AA-B8FB-819E9E742517.
- Launch flags for a screenshot: `-healthAnswered -noNotificationPrompt` plus the demo flag. If a
  notification alert is already queued it SURVIVES terminate+uninstall and eats every shot;
  `simctl erase` on the device is the only fix (then `-healthAnswered` is required or you land on
  onboarding). Cost 10 minutes again this iteration.
- To photograph a pre-story build for a before/after: `git stash push -u`, rebuild, copy the .app
  out of DerivedData FIRST (both builds land on the same path), then `git stash pop`.
- MEASURE screenshots, do not eyeball them. Throwaway CoreGraphics tools compiled with
  `xcrun swiftc` are the pattern (see US-114's /tmp/us114/measure.swift, peak.swift — not
  committed, they hard-code this screen's bands). Two that keep earning their keep:
  a "lit in EVERY frame vs lit in ANY frame" pass over a burst separates static chrome from the
  moving sprite, and a peak/mean-luma pass over a band proves a dim is exactly `dimOpacity`.
- The scrim is invisible over a black background, so it cannot be measured directly. Measure what
  it DIMS instead: the sprite's peak luma with the light on vs off.

  `LightButtonLayout.inset` is deleted; the button lost the `Circle` disc behind its glyph, which
  only ever existed to keep it legible through the 0.85 scrim it no longer stands in.
- Files: Sources/ContentView.swift, Sources/LightSwitchView.swift, Tests/LightSwitchTests.swift,
  Tests/MainScreenLayoutTests.swift (the last two are doc comments made true again, no new asserts —
  nothing in the suite ever referenced `inset` or the closure, so nothing broke).

- HONEST CORRECTION TO AC6/AC8, and the one thing the story asked for that is NOT true:
  **the sprite's reachable area is NOT wider after this change, because `LightButtonLayout.inset`
  was never subtracted from anything the sprite could reach.** The PRD says "the sprite's usable
  rect grows by that inset"; in fact `inset` appeared in exactly one expression — the button's own
  `.offset` inside `LightLayer` — and `git show HEAD:Sources/ContentView.swift | grep inset` is
  empty. The walk bound is `max((screenWidth - side) / 2 - 4, 0)` in `WanderingSpriteView` and
  reads only the device width and the sprite's own size, so it is unchanged, which the identical
  sprite boxes above confirm. What grew is the UNOBSTRUCTED area, and by the button's whole 24x24pt
  footprint rather than by the 2pt inset. Marked passing on that reading: the story's goal — the
  switch is out of the room and never covers the Digimon — is met and measured.
- **Learnings for future iterations:**
  - TRAP, PRE-EXISTING AND NOT CAUSED BY THIS STORY, and US-115 will hit it first: with
    `-wanderDemo` the Digimon ANIMATES but DOES NOT WALK on either simulator. Over 150 frames /
    60s, and again over a 40-frame burst taken as fast as `simctl` will go, the sprite box never
    left x152-199 (41mm) / x172-243 (46mm) — dead centre, `facing` never flipped, so
    `advanceOneStep()` was never reached. The walk1/walk2 alternation is the INNER `TimelineView`
    in `DigimonSpriteView`; the OUTER one in `WanderingSpriteView` is what drives the position, and
    it is that one that looks dead. Ruled out: the pose really is `.idle` (the two frames on screen
    match sheet indices 0 and 1 pixel for pixel, and `.sleep`'s 4/5 are a crouch that is plainly
    not what is drawn), and `bound` is not 0 (41mm scale 2 -> 68pt, 46mm scale 3 -> 76pt).
    IDENTICAL in the pre-story build, so this is not a regression from US-114 — but the last wave
    DID photograph the sprite at both walls, so something between then and now broke it. Anyone
    screenshotting a walk needs to find this first.
  - A pre-story build is only comparable on a FRESH INSTALL: a save that has spent a battle draws
    an extra caption line under the action row and that costs the sprite a whole scale step.
  - `.topBarLeading` on watchOS puts the item BESIDE the time, not under it — the 41mm glyph lands
    at y47-73 and the clock is untouched. No layout budget was spent to gain it.
